What is the "Luteinizing Hormone"?

NUliner seeks to provide better and improved daily living for females. NU has found a hormone variant in our bodies called the Luteinizing Hormone (LH). This hormone detects different signs and signals of the monthly menstrual cycle.

Though the average number of days of the menstrual cycle is 28 days, it differs from each individual’s body. Women’s cycles vary due to many factors such as medication, varying hormone levels and even a person’s stress or emotional state.

Detecting the LH variant, NUliner will provide support in indicating and predicting how far along the individual is along the cycle. the Midpoint of a cycle (approx. Day 14) is when LH variant reaches a rise called a LH Surge. The LH Surge is identified through a sudden rise of LH found in urine or other bodily discharges.

By knowing the midpoint of her cycle, the individual can now approximate and count up the days since her last period (from first day of period) and add the halfway number again in order to get the end of the cycle (menstration) and the next cycle.
